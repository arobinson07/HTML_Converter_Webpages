Converting from Fahrenheit to Celsius


Formula:

Temperature(C) = [Temperature(F)-32] * (5/9)


History

Fahrenheit

Fahrenheit was originally defined in 1724 by a german scientist who based the 0 degree point on the freezing point of a solution made of equal parts ice, water, and amonium chloride. He set 32 as the freezing point of water and placed the boiling point exactly 180 degrees above that point.

Celsius

Unlike Fahrenheit, Celsius is based on the inherit properties of water alone. Its j0 detgree mark is defined as the freezing point of water at sea level. 100 is chosen as the boiling point for simplicity of calculation. Celsius is a commonly used SI unit of measure, far surpassing Fahrenheit in popularity.
